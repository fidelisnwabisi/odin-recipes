Oha Soup Recipe

Description

Oha soup, also known as Ofe Oha, is a traditional Nigerian soup primarily made with Oha leaves, a seasonal leafy vegetable similar to bitter leaf, but more tender. It's a popular dish among the Igbo people of southeastern Nigeria. The soup is typically thickened with cocoyam, Achi, or Ofor and can be garnished with various meats and fish.

Steps

  • Oha leaves
  • Palm oil
  • Meat (beef, goat, or chicken)
  • Stockfish
  • Crayfish
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Salt
  • Ground pepper
  • Thickener (cocoyam paste or achi powder)

Instructions

  1. Wash and season the meat, then cook until tender.
  2. Add stockfish and cook for a few more minutes.
  3. Add palm oil, ground crayfish, and pepper to the pot.
  4. Mix in the thickener and stir until the soup thickens.
  5. Finally, add the oha leaves and simmer for a few minutes.
  6. Serve hot with your favorite swallow (e.g., pounded yam or fufu).
